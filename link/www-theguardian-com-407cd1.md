The Guardian reports that Google has allegedly signed a deal with the US Pentagon allowing the military to use Google’s AI models for classified work, joining other Silicon Valley firms such as OpenAI, xAI, and Anthropic in similar arrangements. The agreement reportedly covers use for “any lawful government purpose,” while also stating the system is not intended for domestic mass surveillance or autonomous weapons without human oversight.

The article notes that the deal has intensified internal backlash at Google, with more than 600 employees signing an open letter urging the company to refuse classified workloads. It also places the deal in the context of Google’s earlier policy changes on AI, including Alphabet’s removal of anti-weapons language from its ethical guidelines and its prior involvement in the Pentagon’s Project Maven program.
