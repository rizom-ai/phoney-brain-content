---
title: Confession
---
Confession functions as a social and moral pressure point: once one person admits something shameful, others are pushed into disclosure, turning the room into a space of forced honesty. Rather than offering catharsis or redemption, confession here operates as exposure, comparison, and self-justification. It strips away protective narratives, destabilizes moral self-image, and reveals how truth-telling can be tense, risky, and incomplete while still reshaping relationships and power dynamics.
