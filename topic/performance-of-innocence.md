The topic examines the modern habit of treating sincere intention as a moral shield. It argues that wanting to be good is not the same as being good, and that sincerity can coexist with manipulation, cruelty, or evasion. What matters is not the flattering story people tell about themselves, but whether they can face responsibility without retreating into a cleaner self-image. Innocence becomes a performance: a mix of selective memory, softened narratives, and image management designed to preserve lovability after the mask slips. This performance is both vanity and survival, a way of staying coherent while avoiding the harder truth of what was done.
