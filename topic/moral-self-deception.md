---
title: Moral Self-Deception
---
This topic examines how people construct and protect a flattering moral self-image, often by rewriting their own motives after the fact. It treats goodness less as a fixed trait than as a fragile story maintained through rationalization, selective memory, denial, and self-justification. Rather than framing moral failure as monstrous or exceptional, it focuses on its ordinary forms: cowardice, guilt, bad faith, and the quiet ways people explain themselves into innocence. The core tension is between what people have done and what they can bear to admit, making self-knowledge and moral honesty the real subject.
