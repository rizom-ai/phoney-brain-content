---
title: Moral Self-Deception
---
This topic examines how people protect a flattering moral self-image by rewriting their motives after the fact. It treats goodness less as a fixed trait than as a fragile story maintained through rationalization, selective memory, denial, and self-justification. Rather than framing moral failure as monstrous or exceptional, it focuses on ordinary people who have done harmful things and want to be seen kindly. Exposure, such as confession or public revelation, becomes a test of how people manage guilt once private failures are brought into the open. The core tension is between the self people believe in and the evidence of what they have done, making self-knowledge and moral honesty the real subject.
