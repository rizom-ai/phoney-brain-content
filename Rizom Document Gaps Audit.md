## Audit Goal
Identify the **reference documents that still need to exist** so Sam and Rizom can work cleanly, separately, and without constant voice confusion.

## Must-have Missing Docs

### 1) Brand Voice Split Guide
A plain-language document that defines:
- **Sam voice** vs **Rizom voice**
- What each entity sounds like
- Words, tones, and topics each one should use or avoid
- Examples of the same idea written in both voices

Why it matters:
- Prevents personal writing from bleeding into agency copy
- Makes editing faster and less subjective

### 2) Positioning & Messaging Framework
A core reference for Rizom’s market positioning, including:
- What Rizom does
- Who it is for
- What makes it different
- The key problems it solves
- The proof points and claims it can safely make

Why it matters:
- Keeps external messaging consistent
- Prevents the brand from becoming a vibes-only soup

### 3) Services & Offer Architecture Doc
A reference that maps:
- Core services
- Deliverables
- Pricing logic or package structure
- What is in scope vs out of scope
- How services should be described publicly

Why it matters:
- Helps sales, proposals, and website copy stay aligned
- Reduces fuzzy client expectations

### 4) Content Governance Guide
A working document for editorial control, including:
- What content belongs to Sam personally
- What belongs to Rizom
- Approval workflow
- Publishing standards
- Rules for repurposing content across entities

Why it matters:
- Essential for keeping ownership and voice clean
- Avoids accidental overlap between personal commentary and agency marketing

### 5) Audience-to-Message Mapping
The personas reference exists, but it should be translated into a usable messaging doc that shows:
- Persona pains
- Desired outcomes
- Best-fit offers
- Objections
- Which entity should speak to them

Why it matters:
- Personas are useless if they don’t drive actual copy and offers
- Clarifies whether a message is for Sam’s audience or Rizom’s clients

## Nice-to-have Docs

### 1) Editorial Calendar by Entity
A content planning system that separates:
- Sam’s personal writing pipeline
- Rizom’s brand/content pipeline
- Cadence, themes, and priorities for each

### 2) Brand Architecture Memo
A short internal note explaining:
- How Sam and Rizom relate
- Which audiences should know about both
- When the relationship should be explicit vs invisible

### 3) Case Study Template
A reusable structure for documenting client work:
- Challenge
- Approach
- Outcome
- Proof
- Lessons

### 4) FAQ / Objection Handling Sheet
Useful for sales and web copy:
- Common client questions
- Common hesitations
- Standard responses

### 5) Terms, Definitions, and Forbidden Words List
A small glossary that prevents sloppy language and ensures consistency across decks, proposals, and web copy.

Likely covers:

Usefulness:

- Needs operational follow-through in concrete docs

### 3) Audience Personas Reference
Likely covers:

Usefulness:
- Helpful research base
- Needs to be converted into messaging, content, and offer decisions

## Priority Recommendation
If only a few docs get created first, do these in order:
1. **Brand Voice Split Guide**
2. **Content Governance Guide**
3. **Positioning & Messaging Framework**
4. **Services & Offer Architecture Doc**

That sequence will do the most to keep Sam and Rizom from stepping on each other’s heels while making the agency easier to run and the writing easier to trust.
